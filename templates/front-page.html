About

Hi! I’m Nicole, a Tacoma, WA native now based in Vancouver, BC where I swapped rain for…slightly more rain. I first dipped my toes into the world of code in the early 2000s by customizing my MySpace profile with just enough HTML to perfect my top 8 and match my layout to whatever Paramore song I had on repeat. That early curiosity snowballed into a lifelong fascination with visual storytelling. After joining my schools' Yearbook Committee and falling headfirst into the world of typography, colour theory, and layout design, I knew I was meant for a creative path. These days, I blend thoughtful design, accessible UX, and clean front-end code to craft digital experiences that are both functional and delightful.

As a neurodivergent designer, I bring a unique lens to every project - one that’s detail-oriented, systems-minded, and driven by empathy. I’m passionate about inclusive, user-centered design that makes people feel seen and supported. Right now, I’m sharpening my front-end dev skills, building a portfolio I’m proud of, and looking for the right team where creativity is loud, accessibility is non-negotiable, and yes - that’s what you get when you let your heart win.
